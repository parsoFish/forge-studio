# Phase: Architect

> *Human-in-the-loop.* Turns ideas into initiatives via interactive Claude Code skills.

## Purpose

Collaborate with the user during ideation sessions to update the project's roadmap and emit one or more **initiatives** — coherent, dependency-ordered collections of features that move a project to a desired state. Once an initiative is confirmed, it's queued for unattended execution; the next human touch is the review loop.

## Inputs

- The user's free-form idea / brief / pain point.
- The project's existing roadmap (`projects/<name>/roadmap.md`).
- Brain knowledge (queried via `brain-query`).
- Past initiatives + retros for the project (`brain/projects/<name>/themes/`).

## Outputs

- Updated `projects/<name>/roadmap.md`.
- One or more `_queue/pending/<initiative-id>.md` manifests with frontmatter:
  ```yaml
  ---
  initiative_id: INIT-<YYYYMMDD>-<slug>
  project: <project-name>
  created_at: <ISO-8601>
  iteration_budget: 50           # max Ralph iterations across the whole initiative
  cost_budget_usd: 25.00         # initiative-level cap
  features:
    - feature_id: FEAT-1
      title: ...
      depends_on: []
    - feature_id: FEAT-2
      title: ...
      depends_on: [FEAT-1]
  ---
  ```
- Frontmatter is followed by the markdown initiative spec (the LLM-Council-confirmed brief).

## Skills

- [`skills/architect/SKILL.md`](../../skills/architect/SKILL.md) — primary interactive skill.
- [`skills/architect-llm-council/SKILL.md`](../../skills/architect-llm-council/SKILL.md) — chained-reviewer pattern (CEO/eng/design/DX critics) that auto-resolves mechanical questions and surfaces only taste decisions to the user. Inspired by gstack's `/autoplan`.

## Success signals

- **Roadmap quality:** initiatives have explicit dependencies and clear acceptance criteria.
- **Council convergence:** the LLM Council reaches agreement on most points without escalating to the user.
- **Downstream execution:** initiatives generated by the architect have a higher unattended completion rate than ad-hoc-written ones (measurable via the developer-loop and review-loop benchmarks once cycles run).
- **Time-to-initiative:** human spends bounded time per ideation session; the council does the rest.

## Benchmark suite

[`benchmarks/architect/`](../../benchmarks/architect/)
- `prompts.json` — sample idea → expected initiative shape (feature count, dependency depth, acceptance-criteria presence).
- `score.ts` — invokes the architect skill against fixtures and scores output structure.

## Known failure modes (to defend against)

- **Agent over-reaches** — turns one idea into 50 features. The architect's prompt caps initiative size and prefers small, releasable initiatives.
- **Vague acceptance criteria** — propagates downstream and breaks the developer loop. The council includes a critic specifically watching for this.
- **Missing dependencies** — leads to parallel work-item collisions. The council includes a dependency-graph critic.
